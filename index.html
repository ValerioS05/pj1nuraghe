Nuraghes in Sardinia

What is a Nuraghe?

Let's find out!

Nuraghes are ancient megalithic structures unique to the island of Sardinia, Italy. These impressive stone towers are emblematic of the Nuragic civilization, which flourished on the island from the Bronze Age (around 18th century BCE) until the Roman conquest in the 2nd century CE. Function: The exact purpose of nuraghes remains a subject of debate among archaeologists, but they are believed to have served multiple functions. These include defensive structures used for protection against invaders or rival groups, residential dwellings for the local population, and possibly ceremonial or religious sites.

The archaic Nuraghe or Proto Nuraghe
Between 1600 B.C. and 1350 B.C., considered the most archaic between the Nuraghes.
"Protonuraghe" is a term used in the field of archaeology to refer to a type of early nuraghe structure found in Sardinia, Italy. The prefix "pro-" in "protonuraghe" indicates that these structures are considered precursors or predecessors to the more elaborate nuraghe towers that are characteristic of the Nuragic civilization.
Protonuraghe structures are generally simpler in construction compared to later nuraghes. They often consist of a single tower or a basic stone structure, lacking the complexity and defensive features seen in later nuraghes. Protonuraghe sites may also include associated features such as enclosures, walls, or other architectural elements.
The study of protonuraghe sites provides valuable insights into the origins and early stages of the Nuragic civilization, shedding light on the architectural techniques, social organization, and cultural practices of ancient Sardinia. These sites are important for understanding the transition from simpler forms of settlement to the more complex fortified structures that characterized later periods of Nuragic civilization.

The classic Nuraghe with ogival tholos
The main differences between a "protonuraghe" and a "classic nuraghe" lie in their architectural complexity, size, and defensive features. Here's a breakdown of the key distinctions:
Architectural Complexity: Protonuraghes are generally simpler in design compared to classic nuraghes. Protonuraghes often consist of a single tower or basic stone structure, whereas classic nuraghes are more elaborate, featuring multiple towers, interconnected rooms, courtyards, and additional defensive walls or structures.
Size: Classic nuraghes tend to be larger in size compared to protonuraghes. While protonuraghes may be relatively small and compact, classic nuraghes can range in size from medium to large, with some towers reaching heights of up to 20 meters (65 feet) or more.
Chronology: Protonuraghes are generally older than classic nuraghes, dating back to the Early Bronze Age (around the 18th to 16th centuries BCE), while classic nuraghes were constructed during later periods of the Bronze Age and Iron Age, spanning from the 15th century BCE to the 2nd century CE.
