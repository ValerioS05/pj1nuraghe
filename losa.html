Nuraghe Losa

The Nuraghe Losa is an ancient megalithic structure located in the central-western part of Sardinia, Italy. It is one of the most well-preserved nuraghes on the island and is considered a significant archaeological site.
The Nuraghe Losa consists of a central tower surrounded by a complex arrangement of walls, chambers, and corridors. The main tower stands at about 13 meters in height and is constructed using large blocks of basalt.
It is thought to have been built in multiple phases over several centuries, with additions and modifications made by subsequent generations.
In addition to the main tower, the site also includes a courtyard and a village settlement, indicating that it may have been a center of social and economic activity during the Nuragic period.
Excavations at Nuraghe Losa have revealed artifacts such as pottery, tools, and decorative items, providing insights into the daily life and culture of the Nuragic people.
The site is open to visitors and is managed as a cultural heritage site by local authorities, offering a glimpse into Sardinia's ancient past.
