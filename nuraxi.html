Su Nuraxi

Su Nuraxi is another remarkable archaeological site located in Sardinia, Italy, and it is one of the most iconic and well-preserved nuraghe complexes on the island. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is situated near the town of Barumini, in the southern part of Sardinia.
The centerpiece of the Su Nuraxi complex is a large nuraghe tower, which stands approximately 15 meters (49 feet) high and is surrounded by a complex system of defensive walls and other structures. The main tower itself consists of several levels, with a central chamber and narrow passageways leading to smaller rooms and staircases.
Su Nuraxi is believed to have been constructed during the Bronze Age, around the 17th to 15th centuries BCE, although some parts of the site may date back even earlier. Like other nuraghe structures, it served various functions, including defense, habitation, and possibly religious ceremonies.
One of the most impressive aspects of Su Nuraxi is its sophisticated defensive architecture. The complex is surrounded by a series of concentric walls made of large stones, creating a formidable barrier against potential attackers. The layout of the site suggests careful planning and strategic design, indicating the importance of Su Nuraxi as a fortified stronghold.
