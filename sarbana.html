Nuraghe Sarbana

The single-tower nuraghe , is made of basalt blocks, The entrance leads into a corridor niche with an architraved trapezoidal door, while on the left is the stairwell and an ogival section. The staircase was used to reach up to the top of the tower, and it is still in use until today. The corridor leads into the central chamber. The nuraghe has a diameter at the base of 12.60 m and, although demolished in the upper parts, it remains 8.60 m high.


To mention also that the Nuraghe is in the proximity of a Byzantine church, the site of the Giants' Tombs, and finally the sacred well of Cherchizzu. The total of the site is known as San Sabina site.
